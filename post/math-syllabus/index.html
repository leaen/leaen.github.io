What is this page?

Over the summer break between 2017 and 2018 I’ve done my best to collate a lot of resources that have been floating around my laptop, desktop and phone and give a structure to my personal study.

As part of that, I also want to make a reasonable attempt to pin down the areas that I want to learn about the most (since unfortunately I won’t be able to look at everything these holidays). Below I outline several topics I do wish to focus on and a few resources I’ve found for each.

I’ll likely update this page as I go to keep track of the many, many, things I find on the internet.

Topics

Linear algebra

MIT 18.06

These lectures are absolutely wonderful. Gilbert Strang is a phenomenal lecturer and beautifully conveys many of the fundamental ideas in Linear Algebra. The lectures can be found on YouTube or MIT’s OCW website here.

A First Course in Linear Algebra

In terms of textbooks, this is one of the best I’ve found. After going through many revisions as part of having a GNU license, this book thoroughly introduces topics with rigour and formal definitions at each step. The online version of the book can be found here.
